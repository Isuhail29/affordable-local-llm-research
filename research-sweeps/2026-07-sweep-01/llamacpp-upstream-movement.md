# llama.cpp upstream movement vs our b10064

Sweep: 2026-07-sweep-01 | Written: 2026-07-19 | Method: GitHub releases page, PR/issue pages, and direct `git log` mining of our local `llama.cpp-master/` clone (deepened to full history since 2026-02-01, HEAD = b10068)

## The framing correction that matters most

**Our b10064 is from July 17, 2026. It is two days old, not months old.**
The [releases page](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/releases) shows b10064 tagged 2026-07-17 15:40 UTC and the current latest is b10068 (2026-07-18). So "what merged after b10064" is a near-empty set, and the real story is the opposite of what we assumed: **the last three months of upstream work are already sitting inside our binary, largely untested by us.** The survey below covers both: the literal 4-commit delta, and the recent-months movement our build already contains plus what is in flight.

## 2. Speculative decoding: the infrastructure exploded in exactly the window E014 missed

E014 tested only `draft-simple` and `ngram-simple` and found net loss. Upstream spent March-July building three fundamentally different drafters, all already in b10064 (dates verified in local git log):

- **MTP (multi-token prediction), merged 2026-05-16** in [#22673](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/22673) with a long fix stream after it: backend sampling for the MTP draft path ([#23287](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23287)), VRAM-leak fix ([#23461](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23461)), D2D copy removal ([#24086](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24086)), and critically **quantizing MoE models with MTP heads fixed 2026-06-25** in [#24986](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24986). MTP is not a separate draft model: the head ships inside the target GGUF, drafts are one cheap extra layer, no vocab-mismatch problem, and the recommended verify batch is tiny (`--spec-type draft-mtp --spec-draft-n-max 2`). [Unsloth's Qwen3.6 guide](https://unsloth.ai/docs/models/qwen3.6) reports 1.4-2.2x on dense and a modest but real **1.15-1.25x on MoE targets**.
- **EAGLE3, merged 2026-06-12** in [#18039](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/18039), with Qwen3.5/3.6 support ([#24593](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24593)), a long-prompt segfault fix ([#24707](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24707)), and documented Qwen3 draft heads ([#24977](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24977)). The PR itself benchmarked Qwen3-30B-A3B + EAGLE3 (on DGX Spark, so numbers do not transfer to us).
- **DFlash (block-diffusion drafter), merged 2026-06-28** in [#22105](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/22105) (design discussion: [#21569](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/discussions/21569)). Drafts an entire block (8-16 tokens) in ONE draft forward pass. Reported up to **8.08x greedy on dense Qwen3-8B**; the PR explicitly warns MoE targets see smaller gains because parallel verification activates the union of experts (exactly our E014 mechanism, now acknowledged upstream). Draft heads are 5-layer models from [z-lab](https://github.com/z-lab/dflash): [z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash](https://huggingface.co/z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash) (~3.2 GB bf16, quantizes to well under 1 GB), [z-lab/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash](https://huggingface.co/z-lab/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash) with a ready GGUF at [abhinand/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/abhinand/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash-GGUF) (**Q4_K_M 292 MB**), and [z-lab/Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B-DFlash](https://huggingface.co/z-lab/Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B-DFlash) (targets the Coder variant, not our Instruct-2507, so head/target mismatch for our current flagship).
- **Instrumentation we should adopt:** per-position acceptance-rate and mean-acceptance-length metrics ([#24536](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24536), 2026-06-16) and a dedicated spec-decode benchmark harness in server-bench ([#23869](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23869)). Both would have made E014 easier to diagnose; both are in b10064.
- Also in b10064: draft-simple crash fix ([#25720](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25720), 2026-07-15, so our E014 runs already had it), auto-download of dflash-/eagle3- sidecar models ([#25811](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25811)), draft-model fit-vs-load fix ([#25056](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25056)), ngram-map out-of-bounds fix ([#23936](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23936)).
- **In flight (not merged): DSpark** in [#25173](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25173), a semi-autoregressive Markov head on top of DFlash, reporting 1.88x overall and 1.21x over DFlash on RTX 4090 Qwen3-8B. Watch for merge; do not build against it yet.

**External falsification datapoint that validates E014:** [thc1006/qwen3.6-speculative-decoding-rtx3090](https://github.com/thc1006/qwen3.6-speculative-decoding-rtx3090) benchmarked 19 spec configs (ngram-cache, ngram-mod, classic draft) on Qwen3.6-35B-A3B on an RTX 3090 and found **no variant achieves net speedup on an A3B MoE** (same conclusion as our E014, on much stronger hardware; discussion mirrored on [the unsloth GGUF repo](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-GGUF/discussions/14)). Crucially they did NOT test MTP or DFlash, the two native-head methods with the best mechanism-level case. That gap is our opening.

## 3. MoE and CPU-path movement: our conclusions still stand

Mining `git log -- ggml/src/ggml-cpu` since April plus all MoE-tagged commits since March:

- **Nothing landed that reopens our closed kernel track.** No sgemm path for mul_mat_id on x86, no new AVX2 MoE kernels, no x86 repack rework. The AVX2 Q6_K optimization ([#22345](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/22345), 2026-04-26) and the CPU RMS_NORM+MUL fusion ([#22423](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/22423), 2026-05-06) are both already in b10064 and were part of the baseline E021-E028 measured. The serious MUL_MAT_ID reworks all went to other backends: Hexagon ([#24954](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24954), [#25425](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25425)), SYCL ([#24452](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24452), [#25217](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25217)), OpenCL/Adreno (steady stream), WebGPU ([#22464](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/22464)). Our finding that the x86 CPU path is near-optimal-and-neglected is consistent with where upstream effort is going.
- **A -ncmoe crash was fixed 2026-07-05** ([#25028](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25028), fixes [#24886](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/24886)) but it is a different bug from ours: tensor-parallel (`-sm tensor`, multi-GPU) + `-ncmoe` tripping a contiguity assert on the mirrored router tensor. Single-GPU hybrid is unaffected.
- **Our b10064 crash (0xC0000005 repacking 3D expert tensors via -ot in hybrid CUDA context) appears UNREPORTED and UNFIXED upstream.** Targeted issue searches found nothing matching; the closest issue, [#23603](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/23603) (CPU_REPACK buffer allocation failure on MoE with mmap, workaround `--no-repack`), was closed as stale without a fix, and `ggml-cpu/repack.cpp` has had no x86 behavioral change since (only warning/RVV fixes [#20692](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20692), [#20682](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20682)). Expect our repro to still fire on b10068. Per project policy (llama.cpp forbids AI-submitted issues), the repro doc lives in our repo only.
- **Hybrid scheduler churn to watch:** "sched: reintroduce less synchronizations during split compute" ([#20793](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20793)) merged 2026-06-26 and was **reverted four days later** ([#25138](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25138)). Split-compute sync overhead is exactly the CPU-GPU handoff path our -ncmoe config lives on. If it re-lands, that is an immediate A/B/A candidate.
- **Tunable we never tested:** the CUDA backend's op-offload threshold is env-tunable, `GGML_OP_OFFLOAD_MIN_BATCH` (default 32; verified present in our b10068 tree, `ggml-cuda.cu` line ~5325). With CPU-resident experts, batches >= threshold get shipped to GPU; this governs hybrid PREFILL economics. The [Doctor-Shotgun MoE offload guide](https://huggingface.co/blog/Doctor-Shotgun/llamacpp-moe-offload-guide) (2026-01-30) pairs it with large `-b 4096 -ub 4096` for big prefill wins on CPU+GPU MoE setups. We have never tuned prefill on the 30B flagship.
- Smaller items already in b10064: `--n-cpu-moe` fixed for fused gate+up models ([#20416](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20416)), llama-bench now prints `-n-cpu-moe` ([#20984](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20984)), CUDA MoE gate/up activation-quant dedup ([#25441](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25441)), mul_mat_vec_q_moe enrolled in PDL ([#24087](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24087), a Blackwell-relevant CUDA micro-opt).

## 4. Model support movement seen through the llama.cpp lens

- **Qwen3.6-35B-A3B is the headline for our rig class.** MoE, A3B active class like our flagship, **multimodal (vision), 256K context** ([unsloth docs](https://unsloth.ai/docs/models/qwen3.6)). GGUFs: [bartowski](https://huggingface.co/bartowski/Qwen_Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-GGUF) and [unsloth MTP variant](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF) with the trained MTP head embedded: **Q4_K_M 22.7 GB** (Q3_K_M 17.1 GB fallback), ~23 GB RAM for 4-bit per unsloth. Community reports it running at ~30 t/s on a 6 GB VRAM machine via expert offload, i.e. our exact playbook ([Medium writeup](https://mychen76.medium.com/run-qwen3-6-35b-a3b-on-6gb-vram-using-llama-cpp-30-tps-a89032e5a60c)).
- **Gemma 4 landed with MTP assistants** (E2B/E4B assistants [#24282](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24282), Gemma4 MTP [#23398](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23398)), though the interesting sizes are dense and would decode too slowly hybrid on our RAM bandwidth.
- **DeepSeek V3.2/V4 support matured** (DSA sparse attention [#23346](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/23346), CPU LIGHTNING_INDEXER op [#24231](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24231), fused hyper-connection ops [#25585](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25585)). Models far too big for 48 GB; relevance is that upstream keeps investing in the CPU op path.
- New small-MoE archs appeared (cohere2-MoE / TINY_AYA [#24260](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24260), sarvam_moe [#20275](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20275)); no evidence yet either beats Qwen3-30B-A3B at our budget. New **Q2_0 quant type** with CPU backend landed 2026-07-07 ([#24448](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24448)); watch for it reaching MoE quants before dreaming about 70B-class fits.

## 5. Server and tooling movement (all in b10064)

- **Router mode / model management**: multi-model serving with on-demand load, LRU eviction, downloads in a dedicated process ([#24834](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24834), [HF blog](https://huggingface.co/blog/ggml-org/model-management-in-llamacpp)). Could collapse Start-30B/Start-8B launchers into one endpoint.
- **Prompt-cache RAM limit** ([#25070](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25070)) plus prompt-cache state refactor ([#25649](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25649)): directly relevant to our RAM-pressure field incident; worth setting explicitly in launchers.
- **Context checkpoints at every user message** ([#24176](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24176)) and server speculative checkpointing ([#19493](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/19493), 2026-04-19).
- **llama-cli was rewritten HTTP-based on top of the server** ([#24948](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/24948), 2026-07-08). This is almost certainly why "b10064 llama-cli hangs headless": it is a brand-new CLI, days old at our install date. Keep using llama-bench/llama-server for measurement.
- New backends elsewhere: Hexagon NPU maturing in-tree, WebGPU maturing, ZenDNN got MUL_MAT_ID ([#21315](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/21315), AMD CPUs only), OpenVINO preview exists as an external backend (Intel; our chip has no NPU, marginal). Nothing here helps an AVX2 Intel + Blackwell laptop.

## Candidate experiments for our rig

Ranked. C1+C2 share one 22.7 GB download.

### C1. Qwen3.6-35B-A3B as new flagship (MODEL upgrade, possibly CAPABILITY via vision)
Download [unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF) Q4_K_M (**22.7 GB, ~2.1 h at 3 MB/s**; Q3_K_M 17.1 GB fallback if RAM headroom worries). Apply the E013 recipe: `-ngl 99`, sweep `-ncmoe`, `-t 12`, mlock, A/B/A flanking vs the 30B flagship. Success bar: >= 25 t/s sustained AND clearly better outputs than Qwen3-30B-A3B-2507 (it is a full generation newer, 256K context, vision-capable). RAM is tight: 22.7 GB pinned + Windows in 48 GB requires the clean-memory protocol. Risk: ~10-15% slower than the 30B from extra weight per token; that is what the bar tests.

### C2. Native-head spec decode on the MoE hybrid: the E014 rematch (SPEED upgrade)
Same download as C1 (MTP head embedded) plus the **292 MB** DFlash head ([abhinand GGUF](https://huggingface.co/abhinand/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash-GGUF)). Three arms on the identical -ncmoe config: baseline vs `--spec-type draft-mtp --spec-draft-n-max {1,2,3}` vs `--spec-type draft-dflash`. Mechanism hypothesis: E014 died on expert-union verification cost with a 16-token draft; MTP at n-max 2 bounds the union to 2 tokens and drafts are nearly free, so the economics are genuinely different. DFlash's 8-16 token blocks should LOSE on CPU experts (prediction registered; upstream agrees MoE gains are smaller). Use the new acceptance-per-position metrics (#24536) to attribute the result either way. External groups tested neither on A3B + consumer hardware: publishable no matter the sign. Use b10068 binaries if KV-quant is in play (#25823).

### C3. DFlash on dense Qwen3-8B, fully on GPU (SPEED upgrade, cheap and fast)
Target model already on disk. Download the [z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash](https://huggingface.co/z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash) head (~3.2 GB bf16; quantize/convert to Q8/Q4 under 1 GB with in-tree tooling, conversion refactored in [#25110](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25110) and tokenizer-mismatch fixed in [#25733](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25733)). Budget: 5.03 GB target + ~1 GB head + KV inside 8 GB VRAM. Baseline 62.17 t/s; PR reports up to 8.08x greedy on exactly this target class. Even 1.5-2x sampled would make the 8B the fastest smart config on the rig and would falsify "spec decode never wins here" with the draft-cost mechanism removed. Watch TDP sharing (E014's dense-8B killer): draft and target both on GPU this time, so the CPU-GPU power split does not apply.

### C4. Hybrid prefill economics: GGML_OP_OFFLOAD_MIN_BATCH x -ub sweep (incremental, zero download)
On the current 30B flagship: sweep `GGML_OP_OFFLOAD_MIN_BATCH` {16, 32 (default), 64, 128} x `-b/-ub` {512, 2048, 4096} and measure PREFILL t/s at 2K/8K/32K prompts, llama-bench pp tests, A/B/A. We have never measured or tuned prefill; the offload guide claims batch size is the critical knob for CPU+GPU MoE. Long-context usability (and any future 256K work from C1) is gated on prefill, not decode. Hours, no downloads, publishable as a tuning map.

### C5. b10068 bump + repack-crash re-verification + watchlist (incremental housekeeping)
Rebuild the instrumented source at b10068 (rebase e027 profiler patch), re-run the -ot 3D expert repack 0xC0000005 repro to confirm it survives #25028 (expected: yes, different bug), and publish the full repro writeup in our repo. Set watches on: DSpark [#25173](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/25173) (merge = extend C2/C3 by one arm), sched sync-reduction re-land ([#20793](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/20793) revert), and Q2_0 MoE quant availability. Hours.

## Bottom line

Upgrading from b10064 buys nothing today (4 commits, none on our path) except the DFlash KV-rotation fix. The breakthrough exposure is not in new builds; it is in the three native-head speculative decoders and the Qwen3.6-35B-A3B MTP model that our two-day-old binary already supports and we have never run.

## Feasibility verdicts

Adversarial review 2026-07-19. Checks: hardware fit (8 GB VRAM / 48 GB RAM / AVX2 / Windows), implementation-exists (verified in our local `llama.cpp-master` tree and on HF), duplication vs E013/E014/E021-E028/E032, and honesty of download/effort estimates.

- **C1 Qwen3.6-35B-A3B flagship: GO.** [unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF) verified live with Q4_K_M 22.7 GB and Q3_K_M 17.1 GB exactly as stated, mmproj files present for vision. 22.7 GB pinned + Windows fits 48 GB only with the clean-memory protocol, and 256K context is NOT reachable in this RAM (KV alone would be tens of GB), so scope context claims to what fits. Download math (about 2.1 h at 3 MB/s) is honest. New model, no overlap with completed work.
- **C2 MTP/DFlash rematch on the MoE hybrid: GO.** Both `draft-mtp` and `draft-dflash` verified as real, substantial implementations in our tree (`common/speculative.cpp`, `docs/speculative.md` lists them in `--spec-type`), so this is runnable code, not paper-only. [abhinand/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/abhinand/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-DFlash-GGUF) verified live, Q4_K_M 292 MB as stated. E014 tested only draft-simple and ngram-simple, so no duplication. One unverified compatibility risk: the abhinand head was likely converted against the base Qwen3.6-35B-A3B, not the unsloth MTP-variant GGUF; confirm the head loads against the MTP target before burning benchmark time.
- **C3 DFlash on dense Qwen3-8B: MAYBE.** Mechanism is sound and the 4.68 GiB target is already on disk, but the doc's link is wrong: `z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash` 404s/401s and the real repo is [z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash-b16](https://huggingface.co/z-lab/Qwen3-8B-DFlash-b16) (model.safetensors 2.1 GB, not 3.2 GB). No prebuilt GGUF of the 8B head exists anywhere on HF (searched 2026-07-19; the community GGUFs cover only 27B and 35B-A3B heads), so this candidate REQUIRES a `convert_hf_to_gguf.py --target-model-dir` run with the Qwen/Qwen3-8B HF checkpoint present locally (tokenizer plus token embeddings per docs/speculative.md), an unbudgeted multi-GB download and a conversion step with known rough edges (see llama.cpp issue #25116, a DFlash draft that fails to load). Upgrade to GO once the conversion path is proven end to end.
- **C4 GGML_OP_OFFLOAD_MIN_BATCH x -ub prefill sweep: GO.** Env var verified in our local tree at `ggml/src/ggml-cuda/ggml-cuda.cu:5325`, default 32, exactly as claimed. Zero download, hours of effort, and prefill is genuinely unmeasured territory: E021-E028 closed the decode kernel track only. Cleanest experiment in the sweep.
- **C5 b10068 bump + repack repro + watchlist: GO.** Housekeeping on infrastructure we already have (instrumented source build, existing repro script). #25028 merged 2026-07-05 and is already inside b10064, so re-verifying our different -ot 3D-repack crash on b10068 is the correct falsification step. Hours estimate honest.

Priority order stands: C4 and C5 first (zero download, immediate), then the shared C1+C2 download, C3 only after its conversion path is de-risked.
